In the following tutorial, you will learn how to create a clock app icon in Picta Graphic for iPhone. 

For starters, you will learn how to set up a simple grid and how to create a simple background. Taking full advatange of the Effects pane, you will learn how to add shading and subtle highlights for the main shape. 

Using some thin text along with some basic vector shape building techiques, you will learn how to create a simplified grid and a tiny bell icon. Finally, you will learn how to create the clock hands and how to easily adjust them using the Rotate Tool. 





##Step 1

Start by tapping the plus button from the top-left corner of your screen to create a new document. Tap the **Create Document** button and then choose the **Grid** document style.

(image: ClockAppIconiPhone1A.jpg)

Focus on the right side of the **Titlebar**, tap that ruler icon to open the **Properties** panel and then tap the **Canvas** pane. Set both **Width** and **Height** to **680 px** and  select **Pixels** for the base unit.

(image: ClockAppIconiPhone1B.jpg)

Re-open the **Properties** panel and this time tap the **Grid** pane. Make sure that the **Snap to Grid** feature is enabled and keep in mind that this is where you should come whenever you wish to enable or disable the grid or the **Snap to Grid**. Finally, open the **Grid Settings** pane, enter **1 px** in both **Spacing** boxes and **0 lines** in the **Subdivisions** box.

(image: ClockAppIconiPhone1C.jpg)



##Step 2

Focus on the **Toolbar**, tap  the stroke color well and drag the **Alpha** slider to **0%**, which will simply make the stroke invisible. 

Pick the **Rectangle  Tool**, focus on your canvas and create a **680 px** square. Tap and drag, and then tap and hold with a second finger to easily create a square.

Make sure that your square covers the entire canvas, keep it selected and tap the **Fill** color well from the **Toolbar**. Tap the **Gradient**  button to replace the fill color with a gradient. Be sure that the **Linear** gradient is selected, set the **Angle** to **-135 degrees** and then move to the gradient color stops. Tap the left one and set its color to **R=195 G=192 B=233**, and then tap the right one and set its color to **R=241 G=240 B=255**. 
(image: ClockAppIconiPhone2.jpg)



##Step 3

Tap the rightmost icon from your Titlebar to open the **Layers** panel. Using the plus button from the bottom of the panel, add a second layer. Make sure this new layer stays selected, as the new objects you are about to create will go here.

(image: ClockAppIconiPhone3A.jpg)

Pick the **Rounded Rectangle Tool** and set the **Radius** to **55**. Create a **256 x 256 px** shape and keep it selected. Focus on the right side of your Titlebar and tap the leftmost icon to open the Appearance panel. Go to the **Effects** pane, select the fill and apply the linear gradient shown below. 

(image: ClockAppIconiPhone3B.jpg)


##Step 4

Make sure that your rounded rectangle is still selected and go to the **Align** pane from the **Properties** panel. Tap the **Horizontal** and the **Vertical** commands to easily bring your selection in the center of the canvas. 

(image: ClockAppIconiPhone4.jpg)



##Step 5

Make sure that your rounded rectangle stay selected, focus on the **Effects** pane and activate the existing **Drop Shadow** effect. 

Drag the **Blur** slider to **0** and then tap the color well that stands for this effect. Set the color to **R=53 G=52 B=92** and drag the **Alpha** slider to **5%**. 

(image: ClockAppIconiPhone5.jpg)
